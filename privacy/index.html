3. Personal Data We Collect

3.1 Data You Provide Directly

When you register for an account or use our Service, we may collect the following personal data:

  • Account information: Name, email address, profile image, and password or authentication credentials.
  • ORCID data: When you authenticate via ORCID, we receive your ORCID iD, name, and any public profile information you have made available in your ORCID record, in accordance with your ORCID privacy settings.
  • Organisation information: If you join or create an organisation on the platform, we collect organisation name, logo, and membership details.
  • Payment information: If you subscribe to a paid tier, payment details are processed by our payment provider. We do not store full credit card numbers on our servers.
  • Communications: Content of messages you send to us, including support requests and feedback.

3.2 Data Collected Automatically

When you access our Service, we automatically collect:

  • Usage data: Pages visited, features used, search queries, API calls, timestamps, and interaction patterns.
  • Technical data: IP address, browser type and version, operating system, device type, and referral source.
  • Cookies and similar technologies: See Section 10 for full details.

3.3 Data in Nanopublications

Nanopublications you create contain metadata that includes your ORCID iD and name as the creator. Nanopublications are published under CC-BY 4.0 and are permanent by design. You should not include personal data of third parties in nanopublications without a lawful basis. We are not responsible for personal data you choose to include in the content of your nanopublications.

5. AI and Automated Processing

Science Live uses artificial intelligence and large language models (LLMs) as part of its knowledge extraction pipeline. This section explains how AI processing relates to your personal data.

5.1 How We Use AI

Our AI processing pipeline transforms research content (scientific papers, datasets, code references) into structured, machine-actionable nanopublications. This involves:

  • Extracting structured knowledge claims from scientific literature
  • Generating metadata and semantic links between concepts
  • Assisting with content verification and quality checks
  • Powering smart search and discovery features

5.2 AI Provider Independence

Science Live is designed to be independent of any single AI provider. We use open-source LLMs and maintain the ability to swap models without dependency on proprietary US or non-European AI services (such as ChatGPT or Claude). This approach aligns with European digital sovereignty principles.

5.3 AI and Personal Data

  • AI processing is applied to research content, not to personal account data.
  • We do not use your personal data (name, email, usage patterns) to train AI models.
  • Research content submitted for AI extraction may be processed by self-hosted or European-hosted LLMs.
  • AI-generated outputs (nanopublications) include cryptographic signatures for transparency and traceability, in alignment with the EU AI Act.

5.4 Automated Decision-Making

We do not use automated decision-making, including profiling, that produces legal effects or similarly significantly affects you (GDPR Article 22). Credit allocations and content quality assessments involve human oversight.

7.1 Important Limitation: Nanopublication Permanence

Published nanopublications are permanent by design and cannot be deleted, even upon account deletion. This is essential to the integrity of the scientific record and citation stability. Your ORCID iD and name will remain associated with nanopublications you have published as creator metadata. This limitation is communicated to you before publication and is necessary for the performance of the contract (publishing FAIR, citable research contributions). If you exercise your right to erasure, we will delete your account data and any unpublished drafts, but published nanopublications will remain accessible.

8. International Data Transfers

Science Live is built on European infrastructure. Your primary data (account information, database records) is stored in the EU (Frankfurt, Germany). Nanopublication data is stored in the decentralised Knowledge Pixels network, with servers primarily in Switzerland (an adequate country under GDPR).

Some sub-processors are based in the United States (Resend, ORCID, GitHub). For these transfers, we rely on Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) and, where available, supplementary measures as recommended by the EDPB. We continuously assess the adequacy of these safeguards.

We do not transfer personal data to countries without an adequate level of data protection unless appropriate safeguards are in place.

11. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data, including:

  • Encryption in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest for database storage
  • Access controls with role-based permissions and the principle of least privilege
  • Daily automated backups with geographically distributed storage
  • Regular security assessments and dependency vulnerability scanning
  • Incident response procedures with notification within 72 hours to the supervisory authority as required by GDPR Article 33

No system can guarantee absolute security. If you become aware of a security vulnerability, please report it to contact@vitenhub.no.
